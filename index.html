Kananga, DR Congo

Kananga is a prominent city in the Democratic Republic of the Congo and serves as the capital of Kasai-Central Province. Formerly known as Luluabourg, it was renamed in 1966 and derives its name from a local Tshiluba word meaning “place of peace.” Located near the Lulua River, Kananga is a key commercial and administrative center with road, rail, and air links to other major cities. The city is also home to universities and educational institutions, making it an important academic hub. Despite facing challenges like infrastructure limitations, Kananga remains vital to the region's cultural, political, and economic development.
